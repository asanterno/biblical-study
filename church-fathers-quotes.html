Early Church Fathers

A Quote Reference, Organized by Topic

The Eucharist
Ignatius of Antiochc. 110 AD

They abstain from Eucharist and prayer, because they do not confess that the Eucharist is the flesh of our Savior Jesus Christ.

Letter to the Smyrnaeans, 7 — written on the way to his own martyrdom, as a disciple of the Apostle John.

Justin Martyrc. 150 AD

We do not receive these as common bread and common drink... the food which is blessed... is the flesh and blood of that Jesus who was made flesh.

First Apology, 66

Irenaeus of Lyonsc. 180 AD

The bread, receiving the invocation of God, is no longer common bread, but the Eucharist, consisting of two realities, earthly and heavenly.

Against Heresies, IV.18.5

Church Authority & the Papacy
Clement of Romec. 96 AD

If any disobey the things which have been said by him through us, let them know that they will involve themselves in transgression and no small danger.

Letter to the Corinthians, 59 — a bishop of Rome intervening authoritatively in a dispute in a distant church.

Irenaeus of Lyonsc. 180 AD

It is a matter of necessity that every Church should agree with this Church, on account of its preeminent authority.

Against Heresies, III.3.2 — referring specifically to the Church at Rome, tracing its bishops back to Peter and Paul.

Cyprian of Carthagec. 251 AD

If a man does not hold to this unity of the Church, does he think that he holds the faith? If he deserts the chair of Peter, is he still confident that he is in the Church?

On the Unity of the Catholic Church, 4

Mary
Justin Martyrc. 155 AD

Eve, being a virgin and undefiled, brought forth disobedience and death; but the Virgin Mary conceived faith and joy.

Dialogue with Trypho, 100 — an early statement of the Mary/New Eve typology.

Origenc. 244 AD

Mary had no other son but Jesus, according to those who think soundly concerning her.

Commentary on Matthew, 10.17 — an early explicit statement affirming Mary's perpetual virginity.

Scripture & Tradition
Irenaeus of Lyonsc. 180 AD

We should flee to the Church, and be brought up in her bosom, and be nourished with the Scriptures of the Lord... the Church has received this preaching and this faith, and, although scattered throughout the whole world, carefully preserves it.

Against Heresies, III.4.1 & III.24.1 — Scripture read within the Church's living Tradition, not apart from it.

Basil the Greatc. 375 AD

Of the beliefs and practices preserved in the Church, some we possess derived from written teaching; others we have received delivered to us secretly, from apostolic tradition.

On the Holy Spirit, 27.66

Augustine of Hippoc. 397 AD

I would not believe in the Gospel, had not the authority of the Catholic Church already moved me to do so.

Against the Letter of Mani, 5.6 — on the Church's role in establishing the canon Protestants and Catholics both now share.
